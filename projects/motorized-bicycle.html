Project Overview

In Progress

This project is a custom motorized bicycle build focused on small engine installation, drivetrain setup, fuel system tuning, mechanical troubleshooting, and performance testing. The goal was to build a functional motor-assisted bicycle while documenting the design decisions, testing results, cost accuracy, project timing, and engineering calculations behind the build.

The calculations on this page are estimates. They are based on measured top speed, assumed total weight, simplified drag assumptions, approximate fuel energy values, and early testing data. These values are not lab-measured results, but they show the method used to estimate performance.

Design Details

Engine and Fuel System

The bike uses a 100cc two-stroke engine kit running a 40:1 gas/oil mixture. The fuel system required tuning and troubleshooting, including carburetor adjustment, spark plug inspection, and fuel delivery checks.

Drivetrain

The bike uses a single-speed drivetrain setup. The drivetrain was selected to favor torque and practical rideability rather than maximum top speed. Early design notes list a 10:44 ratio, which should be verified before being treated as a final documented value.

Air Intake

A velocity stack and air filter setup were considered as part of the intake system. This section can later be expanded with photos, airflow notes, and tuning results.

Power and Horsepower Estimate

This horsepower estimate is based on the approximate power required to overcome aerodynamic drag and rolling resistance at the measured top speed. The calculation uses simplified assumptions, so the result should be treated as an estimate rather than an exact engine dyno measurement.

Speed Conversion



Top speed = 32 mph

1 mile = 1609.34 m
1 hour = 3600 s

Velocity = 32 mi/hr × 1609.34 m/mi × 1 hr/3600 s
Velocity = 14.3 m/s

Total Weight Conversion



Estimated total weight = 200 lb

1 lb = 0.4536 kg

Mass = 200 lb × 0.4536 kg/lb
Mass = 90.72 kg

Rolling Resistance



Rolling resistance force:

F_rolling = Crr × m × g

Assumptions:
Crr = 0.008
m = 90.72 kg
g = 9.81 m/s²

F_rolling = 0.008 × 90.72 × 9.81
F_rolling = 7.12 N

Aerodynamic Drag



Aerodynamic drag force:

F_drag = 1/2 × ρ × CdA × v²

Assumptions:
ρ = 1.225 kg/m³
CdA = 0.60 m²
v = 14.3 m/s

F_drag = 1/2 × 1.225 × 0.60 × 14.3²
F_drag = 75.15 N

Total Force and Power



Total force:

F_total = F_drag + F_rolling
F_total = 75.15 N + 7.12 N
F_total = 82.27 N

Power:

P = F × v
P = 82.27 N × 14.3 m/s
P = 1176 W

Convert watts to horsepower:

1 hp = 746 W

Horsepower = 1176 W / 746 W/hp
Horsepower = 1.58 hp

Final estimate:
Estimated power required at 32 mph ≈ 1.6 hp

Engine Efficiency Estimate

Important Assumption

This efficiency estimate depends heavily on the assumed fuel consumption rate. The whiteboard estimate uses gasoline energy content and a simplified input-power assumption. Because exact fuel flow was not measured during a controlled test, this should be presented as a rough engineering estimate.

Thermal Efficiency Estimate



Gasoline energy content ≈ 34,000,000 J/L

Assumption:
1 liter of gasoline energy used over 1 hour

Input power:

P_in = 34,000,000 J / 3600 s
P_in = 9444.44 W

Estimated output power:

P_out = 1176 W

Efficiency:

η = P_out / P_in × 100
η = 1176 / 9444.44 × 100
η = 12.45%

Final estimate:
Estimated efficiency ≈ 12.5%

Note:
This is only valid under the simplified assumption of 1 L/hr fuel energy input.
A more accurate efficiency calculation would require measured fuel flow during testing.

Troubleshooting and Lessons Learned

Issues Encountered

  • Carburetor tuning required more adjustment than expected.
  • The engine showed signs of rich running, including a black spark plug.
  • Full throttle caused bogging in the upper throttle range.
  • Fuel system routing and sealing required troubleshooting.
  • Exhaust sealing and gasket issues affected early reliability.

Personal Experience Gained
